The Bridge Designer's help system is an excellent resource for exploring advanced features and learning about real-life engineering. Check it out by selecting Help in the main menu. You can select a member by clicking it on the drawing board or by clicking the corresponding entry in the member list on the right-hand side of your screen. Select multiple members by holding down the Ctrl key or by dragging the mouse over the members you want in either the drawing board or member list. Use this technique to delete or change the properties of several members simultaneously. Sort the member list by member number, properties, slenderness, or load test results by clicking the button at the top of the appropriate column. In the drawing board, select multiple members by dragging a box around them with the mouse. Dragging to the right selects members entirely inside the box, while dragging to the left selects members either inside or touching the box. To speed up the design process, run the load test without displaying the animation. Click Test in the main menu, then uncheck Show animation. Delete a joint or member by (1) selecting the object(s) you want to delete, then clicking the delete button on the toolbar, or (2) clicking the erase tool on the tools palette, then clicking each object you want to delete. Use the Go back button to return to a previous design iteration. An iteration is recorded every time you test your design. In the load test animation, bridge deflections are exaggerated by a factor of 10 to help you better see how a truss carries load. Real bridges don't bend this much! Display the Load test animation without exaggerated deflections, color changes in the members, or various background elements by clicking the drop arrow on the Animation controls and clearing the corresponding check box. For a given weight and type of material, hollow tubes are somewhat more expensive than solid bars, but tubes carry compressive member forces much more efficiently. Therefore, it is often better to use tubes for compression members. For a given weight and type of material, hollow tubes and solid bars carry tensile member forces with equal efficiency. Since tubes are somewhat more expensive than bars, it is generally better to use solid bars for tension members. In the Load test animation, tension members turn blue, and compression members turn red. The intensity of color is proportional to the intensity of loading. Bright colors mean the bridge is working hard! In the Load test animation, experience what it's like to ride in the truck while crossing a successful bridge by clicking the faded truck icon and dragging the mouse to look around. If you make a mistake while creating or editing your structural model, click the undo button. You can undo your undo with the redo button. The total cost of your bridge is the sum of four parts: (1) materials, (2) connections, (3) product, and (4) site costs. Click the Report menu and select Cost calculations to see how the total is computed. If you are just learning how to use the Bridge Designer, load a Standard Truss Template to see joint and member positions that create a stable structural model. You can move a joint by selecting it and pressing the appropriate arrow key on your keyboard. The drawing grid has three different resolutions. To ensure the shape of your structural model is symmetrical, switch on the Symmetry Guides in the View menu. Turn on member numbers in the drawing board to match each member with its numbered row in the member list. To do this, select View in the main menu, then Member numbers.
